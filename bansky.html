Bansky

A project centered on the creation of the editorial and graphic material for a fictional exhibition. The deliverables included a catalog, diptych, tickets and posters, conceived as a consistent visual system that captures the raw, urban spirit of the artist while fitting into a museum context.

Concept & Research

The starting point was the analysis of Tate Modern’s visual language and previous exhibition resources, such as catalogs and invitations, to ensure consistency. At the same time, references from urban art publishing guided the development of an editorial style. This led to the choice of perfect binding for the catalog, giving it a professional and contemporary finish.

Visual Identity & Production

The identity was defined by the Gotham typeface and a palette of navy, gray, black and red, striking a balance between clarity and boldness. Retouched imagery with added textures reinforced the underground aesthetic. The final set of pieces —catalog, diptych, tickets and posters— worked together to create a unified and tactile experience.

Result

The project delivers a strong editorial proposal that adapts the language of street art to a cultural institution, achieving a distinctive and coherent system of communication pieces.
