Walk Away

A social graphic campaign that raises awareness about mass deportation in the U.S. and questions current immigration policies. The campaign is designed for both social media and outdoor advertising, aiming to reach a wide audience and spark reflection in digital platforms as well as in public spaces.


Awarded with Silver Laus in the Advertising category for my project "Walk Away". Issued by ADG Laus 2025.

Briefing

Use urban signage as a visual metaphor for deportation, raising awareness about the forced expulsion of immigrants. The posters will be designed to create strong impact both on social media and in public spaces, encouraging reflection on immigration policies and the need for change.

Production

The campaign consists of three posters featuring edited photographs that modify urban signage. These visual interventions recreate common signs (such as traffic signs) to represent the deportation of immigrants in the U.S. It uses the typography and red card colors of the ILRC, ensuring visual coherence.
