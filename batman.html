Chupa chups x Batman

As art director, I led the creative development of a fictional campaign for Chupa Chups in collaboration with DC and the release of Batman (2026). The project aimed to merge two cultural icons—one from the world of candy and the other from comics—into a bold and playful visual identity.

Briefing

The challenge was to design a campaign that connected with families and fans of all ages, celebrating both the nostalgia of Chupa Chups and the iconic imagery of Batman. The goal was to create colorful, dynamic pieces that could live in both urban advertising and digital media.

Concept

The campaign’s concept was built around the idea of “Be Your Own Hero”, positioning Chupa Chups as a symbol of fun, imagination, and empowerment. The visuals integrate classic Batman references with the product itself, bringing the lollipop into action scenes as props, graphic details, or even as part of Gotham’s environment.

Sketches

Art Decoration

The visual language drew heavily from comic book aesthetics, using onomatopoeias, saturated colors, bold lines, and halftone backgrounds. The typography combined Helvetica with Ka-Boom, evoking both clarity and the dynamism of comic culture. Each poster was color-coded by flavor, adding a playful twist while keeping consistency across the series.


The result was a set of illustrated posters and promotional pieces that merge the worlds of Batman and Chupa Chups into a vibrant, intergenerational campaign—nostalgic for adults, exciting for children, and visually striking for all audiences.
